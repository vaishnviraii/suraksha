FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

  • 1.What is Aasra?

    Answer: Aasra is a 24/7 suicide prevention and crisis intervention helpline in India, providing emotional support to individuals feeling overwhelmed, depressed, or in crisis. It aims to help those dealing with suicidal thoughts or mental health issues.

  • 2. How can I contact Aasra?

    Answer: You can contact Aasra by calling their 24/7 helpline at 91-9820466726. They offer immediate support and guidance to individuals in distress.

  • 3. Is Aasra helpline available 24/7?

    Answer: Yes, Aasra is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. You can call them at any time if you or someone you know is in emotional distress or having suicidal thoughts.

  • 4. Is Aasra's helpline free of charge?

    Answer: Yes, Aasra’s helpline is free of charge. There is no cost to call the helpline, and it is open to anyone seeking help.

  • 5.Can I remain anonymous when calling Aasra?

    Answer: Yes, all conversations with Aasra are confidential, and you can choose to remain anonymous. They respect your privacy and will not ask for personal details unless you wish to share them.

  • 6.What kind of issues can Aasra help with?

    Answer: Aasra provides emotional support for a wide range of issues, including suicidal thoughts, depression, anxiety, loneliness, grief, relationship issues, stress, and any other mental health concerns.

  • 7.Who answers the calls at Aasra?

    Answer: Aasra’s helpline is answered by trained volunteers and mental health professionals who are skilled in providing empathetic listening, crisis intervention, and emotional support.

  • 8.Can I call Aasra if I am concerned about someone else?

    Answer: Yes, you can call Aasra if you are worried about someone else's mental health or suspect they might be at risk of suicide. The team will guide you on how to support the person and connect them to professional help.

  • 9.Is Aasra only for people who are feeling suicidal?

    Answer: No, Aasra is available for anyone who is experiencing emotional distress, whether or not they are feeling suicidal. You can reach out if you're feeling overwhelmed, depressed, anxious, or in need of someone to talk to.

  • 10. Does Aasra offer in-person counseling or therapy?

    Answer: Aasra primarily provides crisis intervention and emotional support over the phone. They may refer individuals to professional counselors or mental health services for long-term support, but they do not offer in-person therapy themselves.
