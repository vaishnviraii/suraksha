SUICIDE HELPLINE

"Your lifeline in moments of despair—reach out, we’re here to listen and help."

The Aasra Suicide Helpline is designed to provide 24-hour immediate and emergency support to individuals experiencing mental health crises, especially those contemplating suicide, through a confidential and compassionate helpline. The service offers assistance by connecting callers with trained counselors and linking them to appropriate resources such as mental health professionals, psychiatrists, and support groups based on their needs.

91-9820466726 is a toll-free 24-hour helpline available to anyone in crisis, offering psychological support, guidance, and information to those in need.

Aasra Helpline is integrated with emergency services to ensure individuals in critical situations are connected to immediate assistance, including medical help and emergency response teams.

In the future, the aim is to streamline mental health crisis management through a unified number, 112, for emergency response, and 91-9820466726 for ongoing support and mental health awareness services.

The Aasra Helpline will also provide information about mental health resources, support groups, and connect individuals with relevant authorities and professionals, including psychiatrists, counselors, and community-based mental health workers, depending on their location and specific needs.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS: SUICIDE PREVENTION HELPLINE

  • 1. What is the number for the suicide prevention helpline in India?

    Answer: The national suicide prevention helpline number in India is 91-22-2772 6771 or 91-22-2772 6773.

  • 2. What services does the suicide prevention helpline provide?

    Answer: The helpline offers emotional support, crisis intervention, and guidance for individuals facing mental health issues and suicidal thoughts. It also connects callers to counseling and mental health professionals.

  • 3. Is the suicide prevention helpline available 24/7?

    Answer: Yes, the suicide prevention helpline operates 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

  • 4. Can I remain anonymous when calling the suicide prevention helpline?

    Answer: Yes, you can choose to remain anonymous while calling the helpline. Your privacy and confidentiality will be respected.

  • 5. What should I do if I am having suicidal thoughts?

    Answer: If you are having suicidal thoughts, please contact the suicide prevention helpline immediately. They can provide you with support, guide you through your feelings, and connect you with professionals who can help.

  • 6. How can the suicide prevention helpline assist someone who is feeling hopeless?

    Answer: The helpline can provide a non-judgmental space to talk about your feelings, offer coping strategies, and provide referrals to mental health professionals for further support.

  • 7. Can the suicide prevention helpline help in cases of emotional distress?

    Answer: Yes, the helpline can offer emotional support and help individuals manage distressing emotions through counseling, as well as connect them to additional resources as needed.

  • 8. What if I am worried about a loved one considering suicide?

    Answer: If you are concerned about someone close to you, you can call the suicide prevention helpline for advice on how to approach the situation and provide support to your loved one.

  • 9. What should I do if I feel like I can't talk to anyone about my suicidal thoughts?

    Answer: The helpline offers a confidential and supportive space to talk openly about your feelings. You do not need to worry about judgment or criticism; the focus is on helping you feel heard and supported.

  • 10. How can I access professional counseling through the suicide prevention helpline?

    Answer: The helpline can connect you with trained counselors and mental health professionals who can offer therapeutic support and guide you through recovery.

  • 11. What kind of support can I expect when calling the suicide prevention helpline?

    Answer: The helpline offers empathetic listening, crisis management, coping strategies, and referrals to mental health professionals or rehabilitation centers for continued care.

  • 12. Can the suicide prevention helpline assist with mental health emergencies?

    Answer: Yes, the helpline can provide immediate assistance in mental health emergencies and help you access further support such as hospitalization or therapy if required.

  • 13. What are the signs that someone might need to contact the suicide prevention helpline?

    Answer: Signs include feelings of hopelessness, withdrawal from social circles, extreme mood swings, giving away possessions, or expressing thoughts about death or suicide. If you notice these signs in yourself or someone else, contact the helpline immediately.

  • 14. Is the suicide prevention helpline only for people who have attempted suicide?

    Answer: No, the helpline is for anyone who is struggling with suicidal thoughts, emotional distress, or mental health issues, not just those who have already attempted suicide.

  • 15. Can the suicide prevention helpline assist with long-term mental health support?

    Answer: Yes, the helpline can provide long-term support by referring individuals to counseling services, mental health professionals, or support groups to ensure ongoing care.

  • 16. What should I do if I feel unsafe or unable to reach the suicide prevention helpline?

    Answer: If you are in immediate danger or feel unsafe, please contact emergency services or go to the nearest hospital. You can also ask a trusted friend or family member to help you reach out for support.

  • 17. Are the services of the suicide prevention helpline free of charge?

    Answer: Yes, the services provided by the suicide prevention helpline are free of charge.

  • 18. How can I volunteer or support the suicide prevention helpline?

    Answer: To volunteer or support the suicide prevention helpline, you can contact the helpline directly for volunteer opportunities or donate to organizations that work towards mental health and suicide prevention.
