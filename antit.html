The Maharashtra Anti-Terror Helpline is designed to provide 24-hour immediate and emergency response to individuals reporting or affected by terrorism-related threats and activities. It operates through referrals, connecting callers with law enforcement agencies, emergency response teams, and other relevant authorities to ensure a swift and coordinated response to potential security risks.

112 is a toll-free 24-hour telecom service available to citizens across Maharashtra, offering support for reporting suspicious activities and receiving safety information related to terrorism.

112 Emergency Response is integrated with the Emergency Response Support System (ERSS), a centralized service for all emergency assistance. This ensures that individuals reporting threats can access immediate intervention, whether through police response, bomb disposal units, or coordinated efforts with intelligence agencies.

Looking forward, Maharashtra aims to streamline emergency responses by unifying critical emergency services under 112, enhancing accessibility for citizens needing both immediate assistance and non-emergency guidance related to anti-terror efforts.

The helpline will also disseminate information on government safety programs and initiatives aimed at public awareness and counter-terrorism. It will coordinate with statutory officers under various security legislations to ensure that citizens are connected with protective services and necessary support within their respective areas.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS: WOMEN'S HELPLINE

  • 1. What is the national anti-terror helpline number in India?

    Answer: The national anti-terror helpline number in India is 112.

  • 2. What kind of services does the anti-terror helpline provide?

    Answer: The anti-terror helpline assists with reporting suspicious activities, sharing information on potential threats, and coordinating with law enforcement agencies for emergency response.

  • 3. Is the anti-terror helpline available 24/7?

    Answer: Yes, the anti-terror helpline operates 24 hours a day, 7 days a week to handle emergencies and threat information.

  • 4. Can I remain anonymous when reporting to the anti-terror helpline?

    Answer: Yes, you may choose to remain anonymous when reporting. The helpline respects caller privacy and confidentiality.

  • 5. What should I do if I witness suspicious activities related to terrorism?

    Answer: Immediately report any suspicious activities by calling the anti-terror helpline at 112, which will coordinate with law enforcement for investigation and response.

  • 6. Can the anti-terror helpline assist with bomb threat situations?

    Answer: Yes, the helpline can provide guidance on how to stay safe and connect you with specialized emergency response teams.

  • 7. What should I do if I receive a threat or suspicious message?

    Answer: Report any threats or suspicious messages to the anti-terror helpline immediately, and follow the advice given by security officials.

  • 8. Can the anti-terror helpline provide guidance on cybersecurity threats?

    Answer: Yes, the anti-terror helpline can advise on preventing and responding to cybersecurity threats linked to terrorism.

  • 9. How can I report suspicious activities online related to terrorism?

    Answer: You can report suspicious online activities by calling the anti-terror helpline or visiting a government portal dedicated to reporting cyber threats.

  • 10. Can I receive advice on staying safe during a potential terror threat?

    Answer: The anti-terror helpline offers advice on personal safety and protective measures during a terror threat.

  • 11. Are there specific resources available for high-risk individuals or communities?

    Answer: Yes, the helpline can provide resources and connect high-risk individuals or communities with appropriate safety and security measures.

  • 12. Can I get information about anti-terror training programs through the helpline?

    Answer: The anti-terror helpline can provide information on community awareness and training programs for terror preparedness.

  • 13. What should I do if I suspect a location may be targeted by terrorists?

    Answer: Immediately report your suspicions to the anti-terror helpline, which will alert law enforcement and take necessary precautions.

  • 14. Is support available for persons with disabilities in emergency situations?

    Answer: Yes, the anti-terror helpline provides guidance for persons with disabilities to ensure safety and access to assistance during emergencies.

  • 15. Can the helpline assist with evacuations during a terrorist attack?

    Answer: The helpline can coordinate with emergency services to assist with safe evacuations and shelter options during a terrorist attack.

  • 16. How can I protect myself against online radicalization?

    Answer: Contact the anti-terror helpline for advice on safeguarding against online radicalization and reporting suspicious content.

  • 17. Are the services of the anti-terror helpline free of charge?

    Answer: Yes, all services provided by the anti-terror helpline are free of charge.

  • 18. How can I volunteer to support anti-terror efforts?

    Answer: Contact the anti-terror helpline to inquire about volunteer opportunities or to support organizations focused on public safety and anti-terror initiatives.
