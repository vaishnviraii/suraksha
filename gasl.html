The Gas Leak Helpline is intended to provide 24-hour immediate and emergency response to individuals affected by gas leaks through referral (linking with appropriate authorities such as fire services, police, and emergency response teams) and to provide information about gas safety measures and guidelines across the country through a single uniform number.

1800 is a toll-free 24-hour telecom service made available to individuals seeking support and information about gas-related emergencies. The Gas Leak Helpline is integrated with 112 - Emergency Response Support System (ERSS) for all emergency assistance.

In the future, the endeavour would be to have a single number, 112, for emergency response and 1800 for all non-emergency services and information dissemination related to gas safety.

The Gas Leak Helpline provides a toll-free 24-hour telecom service to individuals seeking support and information by connecting them with Emergency Response Support System (ERSS) for emergency services like fire, police, or ambulance services. It also offers guidelines and safety tips to prevent gas leaks and reduce associated risks.

The helpline will also provide information about gas safety guidelines, emergency procedures, and connect individuals with relevant authorities such as Gas Safety Officers or local service providers as per requirement in their area.

Any individual detecting or experiencing a gas leak in their home, workplace, or public areas is encouraged to contact this number for immediate assistance and guidance.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS: GAS-LEAK HELPLINE

  • 1. What is the number for the gas leak helpline in India?

    Answer: The emergency helpline number for reporting gas leaks in India is 1906.

  • 2. What should I do if I smell gas in my home?

    Answer: If you smell gas, immediately open windows for ventilation, turn off the gas supply, avoid using electrical devices, and call the gas leak helpline at 1906.

  • 3. Is the gas leak helpline available 24/7?

    Answer: Yes, the gas leak helpline is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week to handle emergencies.

  • 4. Can I remain anonymous when reporting a gas leak?

    Answer: Yes, you can choose to remain anonymous when reporting a gas leak. Your privacy and confidentiality will be maintained.

  • 5. What should I do if there is a gas cylinder leak?

    Answer: If there is a gas cylinder leak, turn off the valve, ventilate the area, avoid flames and electrical switches, and call the gas leak helpline for assistance.

  • 6. How can the gas leak helpline assist in case of a major gas leak?

    Answer: The gas leak helpline can dispatch emergency responders, guide you through safety steps, and coordinate with local authorities for containment.

  • 7. What steps should I follow to ensure safety during a gas leak?

    Answer: Immediately ventilate the area, avoid using flames or electrical devices, evacuate the premises if necessary, and contact the helpline for assistance.

  • 8. Can I report gas leaks related to my stove or pipe fittings?

    Answer: Yes, you can report any gas leaks, including those from stoves or pipe fittings, to the helpline for guidance and support.

  • 9. What should I do if I suspect a gas leak at a nearby location?

    Answer: If you suspect a gas leak nearby, contact the gas leak helpline and inform them of the location for further investigation and assistance.

  • 10. Can the gas leak helpline provide information on gas safety measures?

    Answer: Yes, the gas leak helpline can provide general safety tips and advice on how to prevent gas leaks and handle emergencies.

  • 11. Can the gas leak helpline assist with gas safety inspections?

    Answer: While the helpline may guide you on safety practices, contact your local gas provider for formal safety inspections.

  • 12. What should I do if I experience frequent gas leaks?

    Answer: If you experience frequent gas leaks, call your gas provider for a comprehensive check, and contact the helpline for immediate guidance if needed.

  • 13. Can I report gas pipeline leaks through the helpline?

    Answer: Yes, you can report gas pipeline leaks to the helpline, which will coordinate with relevant authorities to address the issue.

  • 14. Are there services available for elderly or disabled individuals during a gas leak?

    Answer: Yes, the helpline can offer additional assistance and guidance to ensure the safety of elderly or disabled individuals during a gas leak.

  • 15. Can the gas leak helpline assist with relocation in case of a major leak?

    Answer: Yes, in severe cases, the helpline may coordinate with local authorities to arrange safe relocation if needed.

  • 16. How can I report cyber-related gas safety issues?

    Answer: If you encounter cyber threats or misinformation regarding gas safety, report it to the helpline or relevant authorities for action.

  • 17. Are the services of the gas leak helpline free of charge?

    Answer: Yes, the services provided by the gas leak helpline are free of charge.

  • 18. How can I support or volunteer with the gas leak helpline services?

    Answer: To volunteer or support gas leak prevention and safety services, contact local safety organizations or inquire directly with the helpline.
