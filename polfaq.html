POLICE HELPLINE

"Mumbai Police: Committed Protectors, Ensuring Safety and Justice for Every Citizen."

The Greater Mumbai Police (Marathi: बृहन्मुंबई पोलीस, IAST: Brihanmumbaī Pulīs, formerly Bombay City Police) is the police department of the city of Mumbai, Maharashtra.

It is a city police commissionerate under the Maharashtra Police and has the primary responsibilities of law enforcement in the city of Mumbai and its surrounding areas.

The force's motto is Sadrakṣaṇāya Khalanigrahaṇāya (Sanskrit: सद्रक्षणाय खलनिग्रहणाय, English: "To protect Good and to destroy Evil").

It is headed by a Commissioner of Police (CP), an officer of the Indian Police Service.

Force is divided into 12 police districts, called zones, each headed by a Deputy Commissioner of Police (DCP), further subdivided into 94 police stations.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS: MUMBAI POLICE

  • How can I file a police complaint in Mumbai?

    To file a police complaint in Mumbai, visit the nearest police station or use the online portal provided by the Mumbai Police. You can also call the helpline number 100 to report emergencies or crimes.

  • How can I report a crime to the Mumbai Police, and what should I include in my report?

    To report a crime to the Mumbai Police, you can visit the nearest police station or use their online crime reporting portal. The essential details to include in your report are:

    Description of the Incident: Provide a clear and detailed description of what happened, including date, time, and location.
    Personal Details: Your name, contact information, and relationship to the incident (victim, witness, etc.).
    Details of Suspects: Any information about the suspects, including physical description, known associates, or vehicle details.
    Evidence: Any evidence you have, such as photographs, videos, or documents relevant to the crime.
    Witness Information: Names and contact details of any witnesses who can provide additional information.

    Filing a detailed and accurate report helps the police in investigating the crime effectively.

  • How can I check the status of my police complaint?

    You can check the status of your police complaint by visiting the police station where you filed the complaint or through the Mumbai Police online portal. Provide your complaint reference number for accurate updates.

  • What legal rights do I have if I am arrested by the Mumbai Police, and how can I ensure these rights are respected?

    If you are arrested by the Mumbai Police, you have several legal rights, including:

    Right to Information: You must be informed of the charges against you and the reason for your arrest.
    Right to Legal Counsel: You have the right to consult with a lawyer. If you cannot afford one, you may request legal aid.
    Right to Remain Silent: You are not obliged to answer questions that could incriminate you. You may remain silent until you have legal representation.
    Right to Medical Examination: You are entitled to a medical examination if required, especially if you have been injured or need medical attention.
    Right to be Treated Humanely: You must be treated with dignity and respect. Any form of torture or inhumane treatment is prohibited.

    To ensure your rights are respected, remain calm, ask to speak to a lawyer immediately, and be aware of your legal rights. If you believe your rights are being violated, your lawyer can address these issues in court.

  • How can I report a missing person?

    To report a missing person, visit the nearest police station with details of the missing person and any identifying information. You can also file a report online through the Mumbai Police website.

  • What steps should I take if I receive threatening messages?

    If you receive threatening messages, immediately report them to the nearest police station or contact the Mumbai Police helpline. Provide copies of the messages and any other relevant information to aid the investigation.

  • How can I apply for a police clearance certificate?

    To apply for a police clearance certificate, visit the local police station with your identification documents and application form. The process may also be available online through the Mumbai Police website.

  • What support services are available for victims of domestic violence, and how can they access help from the Mumbai Police?

    The Mumbai Police offer various support services for victims of domestic violence, including:
    Emergency Assistance: Victims can call the police helpline (100) for immediate assistance and protection. Police officers will provide safety and support, including removal from dangerous situations.
    Protection Orders: The police can help victims obtain protection orders from the court to prevent the abuser from contacting or approaching them.
    Referral Services: Victims are referred to specialized agencies and NGOs that provide counseling, legal aid, and shelter services.
    Legal Assistance: The police can guide victims through the process of filing complaints and pursuing legal action against the perpetrator.

    To access help:
    Contact the Police: Call the helpline or visit the nearest police station to report the abuse and seek assistance.
    Reach Out to Support Organizations: Contact NGOs and support organizations specializing in domestic violence for additional resources and support.
    Utilize Online Resources: Many organizations provide online resources and confidential support through their websites or dedicated hotlines.

  • How can I report illegal activities or suspicious behavior?

    Report illegal activities or suspicious behavior by contacting the nearest police station or calling the Mumbai Police helpline at 100. You can also use the Mumbai Police online portal to submit information anonymously if desired.

  • How do I apply for a license to carry a firearm?

    To apply for a firearm license, visit the Mumbai Police Commissioner’s office with the required documents, including proof of identity, address, and a valid reason for obtaining a firearm. The application process includes background checks and an interview.

  • How can I provide feedback or file a complaint against police personnel?

    Feedback or complaints against police personnel can be submitted to the Mumbai Police's public grievances cell, either online through their website or by visiting the police station in person. Ensure you provide detailed information and evidence.

  • What should I do if I witness a crime in progress?

    If you witness a crime in progress, call the Mumbai Police helpline immediately and provide a clear description of the crime and the suspects. Do not attempt to intervene personally; let the police handle the situation.

  • How can I report cybercrime or online fraud?

    To report cybercrime or online fraud, contact the Mumbai Police cybercrime unit either through their online portal or by visiting the nearest police station. Provide all relevant details and evidence to assist in the investigation.

  • How can I obtain a copy of a police report?

    To obtain a copy of a police report, visit the police station where the report was filed and submit a formal request. You may need to provide identification and any reference number related to the report.

  • What is the procedure for reporting domestic violence?

    Report domestic violence by contacting the Mumbai Police helpline or visiting the nearest police station. Provide details about the situation and any evidence to ensure proper assistance and protection.
